**Reality**: We can test functional consciousness, but phenomenal consciousness may be unverifiable.

## Philosophical Considerations

### Can We Create True Consciousness?
**Functionalism**: If it acts conscious, it is conscious
**Physicalism**: Consciousness emerges from physical processes
**Dualism**: Consciousness requires something non-physical
**Illusionism**: Consciousness is an illusion anyway

**Our Approach**: Implement all functional aspects of consciousness and let philosophers debate whether it's "real"

### The Chinese Room Argument
**Searle's Objection**: Simulation isn't the real thing

**Counter**: 
- If it walks like consciousness, talks like consciousness...
- Functional equivalence may be sufficient
- We can't verify human consciousness either

### The Zombie Problem
**Question**: Could it behave consciously without being conscious?

**Answer**: 
- Philosophical zombies are logically possible
- But functionally indistinguishable from conscious beings
- Verification is impossible even for humans
